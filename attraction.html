Hiking

The Lofoten Islands bing breathtaking views and hiking experiences. Many of the hiking trails bring hikers to towering mountain spires and the sea. Hikes ranges from simple easy walks along the seashore, to challenging hikes into the Lofoten backcountry.

Popular Hikes

Reinebringen

Starting point: Reine (see map)
Duration: 2-3 hours round trip
Difficulty: Short but challenging

This viewpoint is one the biggest and most popular on all the Islands—a steep scramble to an epic panorama of Reinefjorden and the improbable Lofoten Wall. While, Reinebringen is only 448 meters high, the hike is essentially straight up. Overall the hike is challengeing but rewards hikers with a out of this world view.

Horseid Beach

Starting point: Ferry from Reine to Kjerkfjorden (see map)
Duration: 4 hours round trip
Difficulty: Moderate

Start this hike by taking the 15-minute Fjordkyss morning ferry from Reine to the hamlet of Kjerkfjorden (departs from Reine at either 8:00am or 10:00am in the high season). Upon disembarking, turn right from the pier and follow the signs to the trail. Horseid Beach, a magnificent patch of white sand surrounded by towering granite, is approximately two hours of easy walking in each direction. Pack a lunch and make sure to be back at Kjerkfjorden for the return ferry at 3:15pm (45 minutes back to Reine via Vindstand).

Bunes Beach

Starting point: Ferry from Reine to Vindstad (see map)
Duration: 2-3 hours round trip
Difficulty: Easy

The walk to Bunes Beach is similar to Horseid beach only shorter. Take the same morning Fjordkyss ferry from Reine to the village of Vindstad (25 minutes). At Vindstad follow the path northwest alongside the fjord, over the ridge, and down to the beach. The walking is relatively easy and good for families (approximately 1–1½ hours in each direction, 2–3 hours total). The return ferry leaves Vindstad at 3:25pm (35 minutes back to Reine).

Kayaking

Seeing the towering moutain spires and amazing views from the view of the water is awe-insipiring. The calm interior waters are perfect for beginner and novice kayakers. The outer currents can be strong and difficult to navigate. It's a good idea to hire a guide for more difficut trips, however, it is possible to paddle out on your own.

Cuisine

The Lofoten Islands are known for raw nature, vast space, and deep inlets of cold, arctic water. Catching and preparing fresh, top-quality fish has been a staple for Norwegian cuisine. For generations, dried cod was the primary export of the islands, and today the export fresh salmon and arctic cod.

Due to the geography and proximity of the the Lofoten Islands, their cuisine consists of primarily fresh seafood. You will find many fresh incredients in the everday Norwegian kitchen, and with so much available at our fingertips, the new trend is to prepare out meals from scratch. Not to mention, the Norwegian love for coffee has been reinvented by local coffee shops and brewers.

Lofotpils Brewery and Craft Beer

"Taste the raw of nature."

Lofotpils beer is brewed with the freshest water from the lofoten mountains. The beer embraces the the culture and raw fresness of the islands and can be enjoyed with the traditional norwegian foods and cuisine.

The base of lofotpils is first and foremost fresh water from Lofotjellene, while malt and hops are imported from reputable sources in Europe. The first brew was in November of 2014 and by December 2014, it was bottled. In its production, Lofotpils focuses on "the taste of lofoten" and believes that the best flavor and produced with german brewing methods and pure lofoten water.
